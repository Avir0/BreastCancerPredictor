About Breast Cancer
Breast cancer

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that develops in breast cells. It is the most commonly occurring cancer in women and the second most common cancer overall. It can develop in both men and women, but it is far more common in women.

Symptoms may include a lump or mass in the breast, change in breast size or shape, dimpling of the skin, nipple discharge, and redness or flaky skin in the nipple area. Early diagnosis is crucial in improving survival rates.

Types:
  • Ductal carcinoma in situ (DCIS)
  • Invasive ductal carcinoma (IDC)
  • Invasive lobular carcinoma (ILC)
Treatment:

Treatment options vary based on the stage and type, including surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, hormone therapy, and targeted therapy.
